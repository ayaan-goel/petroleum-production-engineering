Artifical Lift
ò
In case the reservoir is unable to furnish sufficient energy to produce
fluids to the surface, artificial lift equipment is used to enhance
production rates by adding energy to the production system.
ò
This component of the system is composed of both surface and
subsurface elements.
ò
This additional energy can be furnished directly to the fluid through
subsurface pumps, by reducing the backpressure at the reservoir with
surface compression equipment to lower wellhead pressure, or by
injecting gas into the production string to reduce the flowing gradient of
the fluid.

Tubulars
ò
Most oil wells produce reservoir fluids through tubing strings.
ò
Production tubing is a wellbore tubular used to produce reservoir fluids.
ò
It forms the conduit for the reservoir fluids to flow from well bore to surface.
ò
Typically, tubing is run inside a casing or liner but can also be cemented in
slim-hole wells as the casing.
ò
Depending on the type of completion, one or two tubing strings may be used
in the well.
ò
Production tubing is assembled with other completion components to make
up the production string.
Gaurav Hazarika, Asst. Professor, PDEU

--- Page 2 ---
03-02-2026
2
Tubulars
ò
Should be compatible with the wellbore geometry, reservoir production
characteristics and the reservoir fluids.
ò
Provide good sealing performance and allow the use of gas expansion to lift
oil.
ò
Gas wells produce gas through tubing strings to reduce liquid loading
problems.
ò
Tubing strings are designed considering tension, collapse, and burst loads
under various well-operating conditions to prevent loss of tubing string
integrity including mechanical failure and deformation due to excessive
stresses and buckling.
Gaurav Hazarika, Asst. Professor, PDEU
Functions of Tubing
ò
It is essential for well killing, circulation and work over.
ò
Tubing provides optimized flow channel to produce optimum/maximum
efficient rate from a well.
ò
Tubing is used to isolate casing from effect of high pressure, high temperature
and corrosive fluids.
ò
It facilitates completion, production and control of flow in multiple commingled
or parallel completion.
ò
It is essential for most common artificial lift operations.
ò
It facilitates the installation of common wire line operated down whole tools.
Gaurav Hazarika, Asst. Professor, PDEU

Packer
ò
Production packers are an integral part of the completion process of an oil or
a gas well.
ò
Provides a means of sealing the tubing string from the casing thereby
preventing communication of fluids.
ò
Casing used in a well is a permanent component of the complete system,
repair/replacement of casing is very complicated and expensive, whereas the
packer along with the tubing string is easier to remove and replace.
ò
Packers are sometimes run when they serve no useful purpose, resulting in
unnecessary initial investment and the possibility of high future removal cost.
Gaurav Hazarika, Asst. Professor, PDEU
Uses of Packer
ò
Routine use of packers should be limited to certain situations:
1. Protection of casing from pressure (including both well and stimulation
pressures) and corrosive fluids
2. Isolation of casing leaks, squeezed perforations, or multiple producing
intervals.
3. Elimination of inefficient "heading" or "surging".
4. Some artificial lift installations.
5. In conjunction with subsurface safety valves.
6. To hold kill fluids or treating fluids in casing annulus.
Gaurav Hazarika, Asst. Professor, PDEU
