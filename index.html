Question 1: Finding Flowing Bottomhole Pressure

Problem Statement

Graphical Solution

Detailed Answer & Analysis

Objective: Find the flowing pressure at the foot (Bottomhole Pressure, BHP) of 13,000 ft of 2 3/8 in. tubing. The well is flowing 100 bbl/day at a Gas-Liquid Ratio (GLR) of 1 mcf/bbl, with a given Tubing Head Pressure (THP) of 200 psi.


Step-by-Step Procedure using the Gilbert Gradient Curve:

  1. Locate THP: Start at the top pressure axis and locate the Tubing Head Pressure of 200 psi (marked as ①).
  2. Find Equivalent Depth: Drop vertically down from 200 psi to intersect the gradient curve corresponding to the given GLR of 1.0 mcf/bbl (marked as ②).
  3. Read the equivalent depth horizontally on the left axis. The equivalent depth for the wellhead is approximately 3,300 ft (marked as ③).
  4. Add Tubing Length: Add the actual length of the tubing to this equivalent depth to find the equivalent depth of the bottom of the well.
    New Equivalent Depth = 3,300 ft + 13,000 ft = 16,300 ft (marked as ④).
  5. Locate New Point: Move horizontally to the right from the new depth of 16,300 ft to intersect the same GLR curve (1.0 mcf/bbl) (marked as ⑤).
  6. Read BHP: Move vertically upward from this intersection point to read the corresponding pressure on the top axis.
Final Answer: The flowing bottomhole pressure is approximately 1850 psi (marked as ⑥).

Question 2: Finding Tubing Head Pressure (THP)

Problem Statement

Graphical Solution

Detailed Answer & Analysis

Objective: Determine the Tubing Head Pressure (THP) for a well completed with 8,000 ft of 2 3/8 in. tubing. The well is flowing at 600 bbl/day, with a GLR of 0.4 mcf/bbl, and the bottomhole flowing pressure is 2200 psi.


Step-by-Step Procedure:

  1. Locate BHP: Start at the top pressure axis and locate the given Bottomhole Pressure of 2200 psi (marked as ①).
  2. Find Equivalent Depth: Drop vertically down to intersect the gradient curve corresponding to the GLR of 0.4 mcf/bbl (marked as ②).
  3. Move horizontally to the left axis to read the equivalent depth. The equivalent depth at the bottom of the well is approximately 12,500 ft (marked as ③).
  4. Subtract Tubing Length: Subtract the actual tubing length from the bottom equivalent depth to find the equivalent depth of the wellhead.
    Wellhead Equivalent Depth = 12,500 ft - 8,000 ft = 4,500 ft (marked as ④).
  5. Locate Wellhead Point: Move horizontally to the right from the new depth of 4,500 ft to intersect the same GLR curve (0.4 mcf/bbl) (marked as ⑤).
  6. Read THP: Move vertically upward from this intersection point to read the pressure on the top axis.
Final Answer: The Tubing Head Pressure (THP) is approximately 600 psi (marked as ⑥).

Question 3: Will the Well Flow?

Problem Statement

Graphical Solution

Detailed Answer & Analysis

Objective: Determine if a well will flow at a desired rate of 400 bbl/day given a PI of 0.4 bbl/day/psi, a static reservoir pressure of 1500 psi, tubing length of 4000 ft, and a GLR of 0.2 mcf/bbl.


Step 1: Calculate the Available Bottomhole Pressure (from Inflow Performance)

Using the Productivity Index (PI) equation: q = PI × (Pr - Pwf)

Rearranging for Pwf (flowing bottomhole pressure): Pwf = Pr - (q / PI)

Available Pwf = 1500 - (400 / 0.4) = 1500 - 1000 = 500 psi


Step 2: Determine the Required Bottomhole Pressure (from Outflow/Tubing Performance)

Assuming a standard Tubing Head Pressure (THP) of 200 psi (as indicated by the solution markings):

  1. Start at THP = 200 psi on the top axis (marked as ①).
  2. Drop vertically to the GLR = 0.2 mcf/bbl curve (marked as ②).
  3. Read the equivalent wellhead depth on the left axis: 7,500 ft (marked as ③).
  4. Add the tubing length (4,000 ft) to find the bottom equivalent depth: 7,500 ft + 4,000 ft = 11,500 ft (marked as ④).
  5. Move right from 11,500 ft to intersect the 0.2 GLR curve (marked as ⑤) and read the pressure vertically on the top axis.
  6. The Required Pwf to lift the fluid to surface against a 200 psi THP is approximately 800 psi.

Step 3: Compare Available vs. Required Pressure

The reservoir can only supply 500 psi at the wellbore to flow 400 bbl/day, but the tubing requires 800 psi to lift that fluid to the surface against a 200 psi THP.

Final Answer: Since Available Pwf (500 psi) < Required Pwf (800 psi), the well will NOT flow at the desired rate of 400 bbl/day.
