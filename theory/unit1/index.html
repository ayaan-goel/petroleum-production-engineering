Q1 Petroleum Production System – Well to GGS 20 Marks
"Discuss about the Petroleum Production System from the wells to the Surface Processing Facility / Group Gathering Station (GGS)?"

🔑 Definition

The Production System is the complete system that transports reservoir fluids from the subsurface reservoir to the surface, processes and treats the fluids, and prepares them for storage and transport to a purchaser / sales point / refinery.

⚙️ Basic Elements (Mention ALL of these)

  • Reservoir – porous & permeable source of crude oil/gas; provides primary energy
  • Wellbore – drilled hole connecting reservoir to surface
  • Tubular goods – tubing, casing, and associated equipment inside wellbore
  • Surface Wellhead / Christmas Tree – pressure control at surface
  • Flowlines – carry produced fluids from wellhead to facilities
  • Separator – separates oil, gas, and water
  • Heater Treater – removes water from crude oil by heat
  • Storage Tanks – temporary crude storage before dispatch
  • Artificial Lift Equipment – when reservoir energy is insufficient

🗺️ Flow Path: Well → GGS

Reservoir → Wellbore → Wellhead → Flowline → Header → Separator → Heater Treater → Storage Tank → GGS / Refinery

🏭 Surface Gathering Systems

The group gathering system consists of flow lines from well(s) to a centralized Group Gathering Station (GGS). Two types:

  • Radial Gathering System – wells arranged radially around the GGS; flowlines radiate outward like spokes
  • Axial Gathering System – wells are arranged linearly along a main pipeline (trunk line); used in elongated field structures
✨ Bluff Tip
  • Mention that the production system is optimized using Nodal Analysis — this impresses the examiner.
  • State: "The production engineer attempts to maximize oil and gas production in a cost-effective manner by optimizing each node of the production system."
  • Draw a simple box-flow diagram: Reservoir → Wellbore → Tubing → Wellhead → Flowline → Separator → GGS
Q2 Types of Gathering Systems 10 Marks
"Discuss with diagram the different types of gathering used in the Upstream Oil and Gas Industry?"

📡 What is a Gathering System?

A gathering system collects produced fluids from individual wells and brings them to a central processing facility (GGS). It consists of flowlines, headers, and trunk lines.

🔵 Type 1: Radial Gathering System

  • Wells are distributed radially around a central GGS
  • Each well has its own dedicated flowline connecting directly to GGS
  • Best suited for circular or compact field areas
  • Simple and easy to monitor individual wells
  • Higher capital cost as each well needs a separate line

🟣 Type 2: Axial (Trunk Line) Gathering System

  • Wells connect to a central trunk line (main pipeline) via lateral flowlines
  • Best suited for elongated or linear field structures
  • More economical for large fields as pipelines are shared
  • Flow direction is predominantly one-directional toward GGS
💡 In exams: Always draw a simple schematic showing wells, flowlines, and GGS for each type. Label everything.
✨ Bluff Tip

Add a line: "The choice of gathering system depends on the field geometry, number of wells, terrain, and economic considerations. In modern fields, a hybrid system combining both radial and axial principles is often employed."

Q3 Role & Activities of a Production Engineer 15 Marks
"Discuss the roles and activities performed by a Petroleum Production Engineer. Analyze and discuss any 5 production activities where the role of a (Chemical/Mechanical/Civil/Computer/Electrical Engineer) plays an integral part."

🎯 Core Role

A Production Engineer's primary role is to maximize oil and gas production in a cost-effective manner. They must have thorough understanding of the entire petroleum production system.

📋 Four Activity Categories (MEMORIZE THIS)

1. Production Optimization (Nodal Analysis)

  • Well Completion, Testing & Services
  • Surface Facilities (Transport & Processing)
  • Pipeline & Platform management

2. Production & Injection Initiation

  • Continuous Production/Injection Maintenance & Monitoring

3. Revival & Enhancement of Productivity

  • Well Stimulation (Acidizing, Fracturing)
  • Water & Gas Control
  • Sand Control
  • Artificial Lift
  • Workover operations

4. Other Activities

  • Field/Well Problem Diagnosis
  • Corrosion Control
  • EOR Facilities
  • Well Maintenance (Hot Oil Circulation, Scrapping, Nitrogen Application)
Fig 2: Production Engineer Activity Categories

🤝 Cross-Discipline Roles (Pick any 5)

  • Chemical Engineer – Corrosion inhibitor selection, scale removal chemicals, EOR chemical flooding design
  • Mechanical Engineer – Design of pumps (SRP, ESP, PCP), compressors, surface separators and vessels
  • Civil Engineer – Platform and pipeline structural design, foundation engineering for offshore structures
  • Computer/Software Engineer – Nodal analysis software, real-time production monitoring dashboards, SCADA systems
  • Electrical Engineer – Power systems for ESP, motor control centers, cathodic protection for pipelines
✨ Bluff Tip

Conclude with: "Modern petroleum production engineering is inherently multidisciplinary. The production engineer acts as the integrator, coordinating with all disciplines to ensure the seamless and safe production of hydrocarbons from reservoir to surface."

Q4 Wellhead Equipment & Christmas Tree 10 Marks
"Draw and demarcate the well head equipment used in the Upstream Oil and Gas Industry? Also discuss which valves are operated so that the oil flow is directed towards the surface facility."

🛠️ What is a Wellhead?

The wellhead is the surface equipment at the top of a well that provides the structural and pressure-containing interface for the drilling and production equipment. It supports the casing strings and controls pressure.

🌲 The Christmas Tree

The Christmas Tree (Xmas Tree) is the assembly of valves, spools, and fittings connected to the top of the wellhead. It controls the flow of wellbore fluids and provides access to the tubing.

Fig 3: Wellhead to Christmas Tree Assembly
Fig 4: Christmas Tree Components Detail

🔧 Key Wellhead Components

  • Casing Head – bottom-most spool; supports the casing strings; provides a pressure seal
  • Tubing Head – supports the tubing string; contains the tubing hanger
  • Tubing Hanger – suspends the production tubing within the wellbore
  • Master Valve (Lower & Upper) – primary shut-off valves on the tubing bore; remain open during production
  • Wing Valve (Flow Wing & Kill Wing) – controls flow of produced fluids to the flowline; Flow Wing Valve is opened to direct oil to surface facility
  • Choke – restricts and controls the flow rate of produced fluids
  • Crown Valve – topmost valve; used during wireline/workover operations
🔑 Valves Opened for Flow: Upper Master Valve → Wing Valve (Flow Wing) → Choke → Flowline to GGS. The Kill Wing Valve remains CLOSED during normal production.
✨ Bluff Tip
  • Mention "Surface Safety Valve (SSV)" – automatically shuts in the well in emergencies using a fail-close mechanism. This is a great add-on detail.
  • State that the choke is critical because it protects surface equipment from high wellbore pressure while regulating production rate.
