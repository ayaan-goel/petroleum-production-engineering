Topic 3 Floating Production Systems

These have a floating structure moored to the seabed. Used in deep/ultra-deep water where bottom-supported structures are not feasible.

Fig 3: TLP & Semi-submersible

ðŸŒŠ Type 1: Tension Leg Platform (TLP)

  • Large hollow hull structure (similar to semi-sub) held by high-strength steel tethers (tendons) at each corner
  • Hull provides high buoyancy, keeping tethers under constant tension
  • Vertically moored â€” no heave, pitch, or roll; only surge, sway, and tilt
  • Used in deep water for production

ðŸŒŠ Type 2: Semi-Submersible

  • Typically four columns connected at bottom by pontoons; truss structure connects column tops and supports topsides
  • Columns supported by two parallel pontoons or a ring pontoon
  • Pontoons sized for adequate buoyancy and to maximize heave damping
  • High degree of flexibility in fabrication; used for drilling and production
